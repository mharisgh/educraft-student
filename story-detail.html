Smart Plant Watering System

Created by

Sarah Khan . Grade 8

Category

Robotics

Published

24 March 2026

Key Summary

I built a smart watering system that checks soil moisture and waters the plant only when it needs help. The goal was to stop both overwatering and underwatering at home while learning how sensors, code, and small hardware parts work together.

The Idea

I noticed that plants at home were sometimes watered too early and sometimes too late. I wanted to make something simple that could read the soil condition and make a better decision than guessing every day.

What I Built

I used an Arduino board, a moisture sensor, jumper wires, and a small pump concept to design a basic smart plant helper. When the soil becomes dry, the system can trigger watering. I also added a simple status light idea so it is easier to understand what the system is doing.

Arduino Create Moisture Sensor Jumper Wires Breadboard

What I Learned

I learned how sensors collect real-world data, how code can respond to changing conditions, and how important testing is when small hardware parts are connected together. I also learned to improve my idea step by step instead of trying to make everything perfect on the first try.

Teacher's Note

"Sarah showed exceptional problem-solving skills and dedication. She troubleshooted issues independently and even added extra features beyond the original project requirements. Her presentation inspired other students to think about real-world applications of technology."
